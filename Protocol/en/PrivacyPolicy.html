1. How We Ask for Permissions

 1.1 Photo Album Access 

When accessing the app, you will be required for your consent, by means of a pop-up, to enable us to index your photos and videos help you search for them quickly using natural language. Note that we will never access your local photo album without your permission, nor will we use any illegal methods to collect information or send ads. Our app can be used offline, so you don`t has to worry about information being uploaded to the cloud. For more details, see section 1.3 about network permissions. 

1.2 Camera Access

If you use the app`s “search by image” feature, we will request access to your camera, by means of a pop-up, so you can take a photo to search for similar images. Note that we will never turn on your microphone or camera without your explicit permission. 

1.3 Network Access 

We may ask for permission to use your network when you`re using the app, by means of a pop-up, to enable us to collect data like crash reports, app identifiers (such as Crashlytics Installation UUID or Firebase Installation ID), and log data. Note that this data is processed as breakdown data (strictly device identifier information), meaning it is not linked to any specific user or personal information. It only helps us understand how users, in general, are using the app.

1.4 You can enable or disable these permissions at any time, through your device settings.
